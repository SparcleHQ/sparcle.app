HOW IT WORKS · 7 LAYERS
Numbered to the diagram. The architecture, layer by layer.
  1. 1Bolt — desktop superagent. Cmd-Space launcher runs on the user's own desktop. Queries are identity-bound. No data leaves the machine to start the search.
  2. 2Three connector tiers. Deep-integration sources (~25) preserve source-fidelity ACL plus per-source enrichment. Standard in-house connectors (~80). Airbyte bridge (~350) for long-tail breadth. Depth matters where your domain authority signals live.
  3. 3Enricher chain at ingest. Per-source rules pull domain metadata into authority signals. FHIR evidence levels, classification markings, regulatory-source tiers. Your domain knowledge enters the search index through this channel.
  4. 4Authority Policy SDK. The customer-extensible ranking layer. Reference impls ship for general enterprise, regulated industries, and healthcare. Vertical packs for finance, defense, and legal are co-developed by engagement. The trust model is written in customer code (Rust trait or YAML rules) and runs inside the customer perimeter.
  5. 5Patent-pending 4-axis ACL. Country, office, role-band, and employment-type pre-filter at index time. The ACL layer is platform-locked. The Authority Policy ranks on top, on documents the ACL already authorized.
  6. 6Sealed Merkle audit chain. Every query, every result, every score, every policy decision. Ed25519-signed. A standalone verifier binary lets an auditor or regulator verify the chain independently, without vendor cooperation.
  7. 7BYO everything. LLM, IdP, MCP tools, KMS. Customer choice at every external boundary. Outside the perimeter, only PII-masked prompts cross to the customer-chosen LLM vendor. On every self-hosted Bolt tier, Sparcle is not in the data path.
